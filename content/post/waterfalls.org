We did the Canada side 9 years ago when we did our Erie Canal bike
trip (you can read about that by checking out some of my early [[https://cestlaz.github.io/posts/2010-08-05-erie-canal-day-1.html/][blog
posts]]). This time, the American side. My take is that the Canadian
side is more polished and all private attractions - think Times Square
or Disney. On the American side, the town itself is somewhat run down
but the attractions are all state park making them well maintained and
affordable. We did the "touristy stuff" - Maid of the Mist and Cave of
the winds, both of which were well worth it and also things like
[[https://parks.ny.gov/parks/105/details.aspx][Whirlpool State Park]] and the [[https://www.nypa.gov/][Niagara Power Project]] along with a few
other attractions. We got a nice dinner at the Niagara Culinary
Institute and an okay one in [[https://www.tripadvisor.com/Restaurants-g48050-Lewiston_New_York.html][Lewiston]]. There were a few things we
didn't get to do and I'm not sure there's enough to merit a return
trip but we really enjoyed the day and a half we spent at the falls.

Lots of cool history from both the war of 1812, revolution  and the French and
Indian war. We're such a young nation so it was cool to see standing
structures from pre-colonial times.  

From there we drove in to Canada. On [[https://twitter.com/dougpete][Doug Peterson]]'s suggestion we
stopped at the [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Welland_Canal][Welland Canal]] which connects Lake Ontario to Lake
Erie. It's much wider than our own [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erie_Canal][Erie Canal]] and ultimately connects
the midwest ultimately to the port of Montreal or beyond.

It didn't disappoint. We didn't get to the two biggest falls - Webster
and Tews but we spent a long day of walking the trails and did get to
see six or seven of them including [[https://tourismhamilton.com/devils-punchbowl][the Devil's Punchbowl]], [[https://tourismhamilton.com/albion-falls][Albion
Falls]], and [[https://tourismhamilton.com/felkers-falls][Felker's Falls]]. It's pretty cool that we're still in the
city but are on these hiking trails. We could probably have done
another day or two just hiking the rest of the falls and another day
to see other local attractions like the botanical gardens.

Our last stop was [[https://parks.ny.gov/parks/79/details.aspx][Letchworth State Park.]]

Letchworth is truly a hidden gem just as the Ithaca area state parks -
Treman, Buttermilk Falls, and Taughannock but more so.

Beautiful waterfall and gorge views. You can go on a hardcore hike or
just drive from view to view. At the south end you've got the three
major waterfalls and at the north end the Mount Morris Dam. There's
also some pretty good food at the Iris Inn which is at the south end
of the park. We didn't stay there but if we go back, I think we will.

If you haven't been and get a chance, you won't be
disappointed. There's a reason Letchworth is called the Grand Canyon
of the East. Not nearly as big or majestic but just as beautiful in
it's own right.
